Phase 01
Pre-Construction Intelligence

Plan with data.
Not assumptions.

Most road construction problems are baked in before the first machine arrives. Width measurements that miss obstructions. BOQ estimates built on outdated surveys. Clearance approvals delayed because no baseline documentation exists.

Cognecto's pre-construction module solves this at the point where it is cheapest to fix — before construction begins. AI-powered field surveys produce width profiles accurate to ±5cm, detect obstructions at the chainage level, establish a baseline that becomes the reference for every quality check that follows, and feed directly into the PMIS for BOQ setup and resource planning.

Road width profiling — AI-measured, ±5cm precision across entire route
Obstruction detection — poles, trees, encroachments mapped at chainage level
KML output — directly compatible with OMMAS and NRIDA reporting systems
Baseline documentation — geo-tagged, timestamped, court-admissible

PMIS · Project Setup
Centralised Project Creation
Define scope, timelines, budgets, and teams for every package in one system. Role-based access assigns accountability from field engineer to Programme Director. All drawings, approvals, and reference documents in a structured repository linked to chainage and BOQ line.
BOQ · Cost Control
BOQ Creation & Real-Time Tracking
Import BOQ line items directly from Excel or ERP. Every line item is linked to the physical work on the ground — when Boson verifies a compaction sequence or Photon confirms a layer, the BOQ item moves from pending to verified automatically. Variances flag immediately, not at month-end review.
Milestones · Schedule
Milestone Tracking & Delay Alerts
Set critical deliverables and deadlines for each construction package. Real-time dashboards show physical and financial progress with % completion indicators. When a milestone risks delay, automated escalations reach the right team — not after a site visit, but the moment the data diverges from plan.
Asset Allocation · Fleet
Equipment Allocation & Health Scoring
Assign machinery based on site demand, utilisation data, and shift schedules. Health scoring from IoT sensors rates equipment condition continuously — breakdowns are flagged before they stop production. 20–25% increase in asset lifecycle achieved across UPRRDA deployments.

Standardised BOQ Templates
Pre-built templates for every PMGSY construction method — GSB, WBM, BC, CC — reduce setup time by 35% and eliminate estimation inconsistencies across packages. Import, customise, and deploy. All templates carry audit-ready cost history and traceability for compliance.
Reporting · OMMAS · NRIDA
Automated Reports & System Integration
Generate chainage reports, width profiles, defect summaries, and KML maps automatically — formatted for OMMAS upload and Ministry of Rural Development reporting standards. Stakeholders at every level — PIU, SRRDA, NRIDA — see the same verified data without manual compilation.

Construction Monitoring & AI Supervision

Every layer verified
before the next one begins.

Construction quality is decided in the 30 minutes after each layer is laid — when the roller is still on site and the concrete is still workable. After that, the defect is sealed in.

Cognecto's construction monitoring layer combines Boson's IoT sensor network with Photon's vision intelligence to verify machinery compliance, count compaction passes per chainage, confirm layer sequences, and detect spread anomalies — all in real time. On Package 6152, AI alerts enabled same-day corrective action, reducing rework costs by 40% and preventing quality failures that would have been undetectable once the surface course was laid.

Spread · Quality
Cement Spread Uniformity Detection
Photon's models identify spread thickness anomalies and coverage gaps in real time as the cement spreader moves. Deviation from specification is geo-tagged and timestamped before the roller arrives. No post-pour inspection. No disputed records.
Progress · BOQ
Visual Progress Recording vs BOQ
AI-interpreted evidence of what was built, when, and by which assets. Layer completion logged against BOQ line items, flagging incomplete work before the next phase begins. For PMGSY and NHAI audits, this replaces manually assembled inspection reports that can be challenged.
Fleet · Machinery
Machinery Compliance & Fleet Tracking
Every machine on the corridor — rollers, graders, tippers — tracked by GPS in real time. Boson verifies that correct equipment is deployed per the BOQ, validates operational sequences, and tracks idle time. Non-compliance triggers an alert before the shift report is filed.
Safety · HSE
On-Site Safety & PPE Monitoring
Photon monitors PPE compliance (hardhats, vests, boots) on every worker entering every zone. No-go perimeters enforced around crane swing radii and excavation edges. Every safety event timestamped and stored as an immutable record — replacing paper-based inspection rounds entirely.

Phase 03
Post-Construction Assessment & Condition Rating

The road is built.
The intelligence stays active.

Handover is not the end of the data story — it is the beginning of the maintenance one. A road that scores 95/100 at handover and 40/100 five years later failed somewhere in the middle. Without a continuous monitoring baseline, you find out when it fails, not before.

Cognecto's post-construction layer transitions from a quality control tool to an asset health monitor. The same cameras that verified construction now scan for cracking patterns, pothole formation, rutting, and edge ravelling — classified by severity, geo-tagged to chainage, and fed into a prioritised maintenance queue. Programmes like PMGSY now have a maintenance baseline that did not exist before construction ended.

Does Cognecto integrate with OMMAS and NRIDA reporting systems?+
Yes. All outputs — width profiles, KML maps, chainage reports, defect summaries, and BOQ verification records — are formatted for OMMAS upload and NRIDA Ministry of Rural Development reporting standards. The platform is Make in India certified and fully compliant with government data security protocols. Field data flows from engineer to Ministry without manual compilation or reformatting.
How does compaction pass verification work across multiple lanes?+
Boson GPS devices on the roller track position and movement in real time. The physics AI layer counts each pass per chainage segment, per lane — left, centre, and right carriageway — and validates the sequence and count against the compaction schedule in the BOQ. Under-rolling and over-rolling are flagged immediately. The output is a geo-tagged pass map that replaces manual tally sheets and is stored as an immutable audit record.
Can the platform handle multiple packages across different states simultaneously?+
Yes. Cognecto is built for multi-site, multi-state deployment. Each package is configured as a separate workgroup within the platform — its own BOQ, milestone schedule, device assignments, and access permissions. Programme directors see the full portfolio. PIU supervisors see their packages. SRRDA officials see their state. All from one Cortex instance, with no data mixing across workgroups.
What cameras and GPS devices does Cognecto work with?+
Boson connects to 20+ OEM telematics manufacturers — TATA Advance, 808GPS, Mahindra, Fleetedge, and custom TCP/MQTT devices — without hardware replacement. Photon integrates with existing IP cameras via RTSP, RTMP, and ONVIF protocols (Hikvision, Dahua, Axis, and generic IP cameras). If equipment is already on site, Cognecto connects to it. Typical activation: 7 days from agreement to live monitoring.
How is post-construction defect data used in maintenance planning?+
Every defect detected post-construction — crack, pothole, rutting, or edge ravelling — is classified by severity, geo-tagged to chainage, and stored in the Cortex knowledge graph as a structured maintenance record. The co-pilot aggregates these into a Road Condition Index by segment and surfaces a prioritised maintenance queue. Programme managers see which chainage segments require urgent intervention and which are within acceptable parameters — before a reactive repair becomes an emergency one.
